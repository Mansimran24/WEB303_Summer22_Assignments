  • Doraemon is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Fujiko F. Fujio. The manga was first serialized in December 1969, with its 1,345 individual chapters compiled into 45 tankōbon volumes and published by Shogakukan from 1970 to 1996.
    Reference: -Wikipedia

  • Doraemon (ドラえもん?) Seiyū: Kōsei Tomita → Masako Nozawa (1973), Nobuyo Ōyama (1979~March 2005), Wasabi Mizuta (April 2005~) Seiyū (Golden): Kazue Takahashi → Chisa Yokoyama Doraemon, the primary character of series, is the titular robotic cat sent back in time by Sewashi to aid Nobita.
    Reference: -Wikipedia

  • In addition to Gian, Suneo and Shizuka, Dorami and Hidetoshi Dekisugi (common name Dekisugi) occasionally joins. Dorami is Doraemon's younger sister, and Dekisugi is a gifted student boy who as Shizuka's close friend so often attracted the jealousy of Nobita. Doraemon: Nobita and the Steel Troops (ドラえもん のび太と鉄人兵団, Doraemon Nobita to Tetsujin Heidan) is a 1986 anime science fiction film based on the seventh volume of the same name of the Doraemon Long Stories series. The original plot was written by Fujiko F. Fujio.

Stand by Me Doraemon (STAND BY ME ドラえもん) is a 2014 Japanese 3D computer-animated science fiction comedy drama film based on the Doraemon manga series and directed by Ryūichi Yagi and Takashi Yamazaki. It was released on 8 August 2014. It is the highest-grossing film of the Doraemon franchise. Bang Zoom! Reference: YouTube
